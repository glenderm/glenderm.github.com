Doctors

Justine H. Park, M.D.

Dr. Park received her bachelor's degree at the University of California, Berkeley and her medical degree from the University of Southern California (USC), where she was elected to the Alpha Omega Alpha Medical Honor Society. After completing her internship in internal medicine at White Memorial Medical Center, Dr. Park went on to finish her residency training in dermatology at Martin Luther King-Drew Medical Center, where she served as Chief Resident.

After completing her residency, Dr. Park became an Assistant Professor of Dermatology and now serves as the Director of Pediatric Dermatology at Children's Hospital in Los Angeles. She also oversees the Resident Aesthetic Clinic at USC, where residents learn about the latest cosmetic procedures, including sclerotherapy, dermal filters, Botox®, chemical peels, and laser surgery.

Dr Park emphasizes empathy and one-on-one patient education in the care she provides so that patients gain a better understanding of their conditions and treatments. Her ability to relate to people of widely differing backgrounds stems from the cultural diversity that she was exposed to growing up in Los Angeles and San Francisco.

Dr. Park speaks English and medical Spanish. She currently lives in West Los Angeles with her husband, Jun, and son, Ethan.

Dana B. Kang, M.D.

Dr. Kang graduated Summa Cum Laude from Amherst College and received her medical degree from the University of California, San Francisco (UCSF). At UCSF, she gained extensive research experience working alongside Dr. John Koo, a world-renowned leader in treatments for psoriasis and atopic dermatitis. After completing her internship at Santa Clara Valley Medical Center, Dr. Kang attended the University of Southern California (USC), where she completed her residency training in dermatology and served as chief resident.

Currently, Dr. Kang is an Assistant Clinical Professor of Dermatology at USC, where she received an award for excellence in teaching. She instructs residents on surgical and cosmetic dermatology procedures, including lasers, chemical peels, and sclerotherapy.

Within dermatology, Dr. Kang has particular interests in dermatologic surgery as well as treatments for severe psoriasis and atopic dermatitis. She has had much experience in using medications for these conditions beyond topical treatments, including oral methotrexate and cyclosporine as well as newer biologics such as Humira and Enbrel.

Dr. Kang speaks English, Spanish, and Korean. Her hobbies include reading, cooking, trying out new restaurants, and traveling.
